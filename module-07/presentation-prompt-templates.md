# Presentation Prompt Templates

## Module 07: AI for Daily Productivity — Code and Examples

---

A collection of 20 complete, ready-to-use presentation prompt templates covering the most common business scenarios. Copy, customize the bracketed fields, and generate professional presentations in minutes.

## Template 1: Sales Pitch to New Prospect

```
Create a 12-slide sales pitch presentation.

Product/Service: [NAME AND DESCRIPTION]
Prospect company: [NAME, INDUSTRY, SIZE]
Prospect contact: [NAME AND TITLE]
Their primary pain point: [SPECIFIC PROBLEM THEY FACE]
Our solution: [HOW WE SOLVE IT]
Key differentiator: [WHAT MAKES US UNIQUE]
Price range: [PRICING INFO]
Competitor they might be comparing: [COMPETITOR NAME]

Slide structure:
1. Title slide — Company name, logo description, tagline
2. The Problem — Paint the pain point vividly with data
3. The Cost of Inaction — What happens if they do nothing ($$$ impact)
4. Our Solution — High-level overview with visual diagram description
5. How It Works — 3-step process flow
6. Feature 1 — Deep dive on most relevant feature for this prospect
7. Feature 2 — Deep dive on second most relevant feature
8. Feature 3 — Deep dive on third feature
9. Case Study — Similar client, before/after metrics
10. ROI Calculator — Expected return for their specific situation
11. Pricing and Packages — Clear pricing table with recommended option highlighted
12. Next Steps — Specific CTA (demo, trial, meeting) with contact info

For each slide provide:
- Title (max 8 words)
- 3-4 bullet points (max 10 words each)
- Speaker notes (60-80 words)
- Visual suggestion
- Transition to next slide
```

## Template 2: Quarterly Business Review (QBR)

```
Create a 15-slide Quarterly Business Review presentation.

Quarter: [Q1/Q2/Q3/Q4 YEAR]
Department: [NAME]
Presenter: [YOUR NAME AND TITLE]
Audience: [EXECUTIVES / BOARD / LEADERSHIP TEAM]
Company name: [NAME]

Key data:
- Revenue: [THIS QUARTER] vs [LAST QUARTER] vs [SAME QUARTER LAST YEAR]
- Key KPIs: [LIST 5-7 METRICS WITH VALUES]
- Headcount: [CURRENT TEAM SIZE AND CHANGES]
- Budget: [SPENT vs ALLOCATED]
- Customer metrics: [NPS, CHURN, NEW CUSTOMERS, ETC.]

Highlights: [3-5 MAJOR WINS]
Challenges: [2-3 ISSUES FACED]
Next quarter priorities: [3-5 PRIORITIES]

Slide structure:
1. Title slide with quarter and date
2. Executive Summary — 4-5 key takeaways in bullet format
3. Revenue Performance — Chart: quarterly trend with YoY comparison
4. KPI Dashboard — Table: all KPIs with color-coded status (green/yellow/red)
5. Top Achievement #1 — Details and business impact
6. Top Achievement #2 — Details and business impact
7. Top Achievement #3 — Details and business impact
8. Customer Metrics — NPS trend, satisfaction, retention rates
9. Team Update — Hires, departures, organizational changes
10. Budget Review — Planned vs actual spending breakdown
11. Challenges and Lessons — What went wrong and what we learned
12. Competitive Landscape — Key market changes and our positioning
13. Q[NEXT] Priorities — Top 5 priorities with owners and deadlines
14. Resource Requests — What we need to succeed next quarter
15. Q&A / Discussion — Key questions for the audience to consider

Include speaker notes that prepare me for tough executive questions.
```

## Template 4: Conference / Industry Talk

```
Create a 25-slide conference presentation.

Talk title: [TITLE]
Conference: [NAME AND TYPE]
Speaker: [YOUR NAME, TITLE, AND SHORT BIO]
Duration: [MINUTES]
Audience: [SIZE AND DESCRIPTION — EXPERTISE LEVEL]
Talk type: [KEYNOTE / BREAKOUT / PANEL / WORKSHOP]

Core thesis: [THE ONE IDEA YOUR TALK IS BUILT AROUND]
Three supporting arguments:
1. [ARGUMENT 1]
2. [ARGUMENT 2]
3. [ARGUMENT 3]

Personal story/experience: [RELEVANT ANECDOTE YOU CAN SHARE]
Data to include: [STATISTICS OR RESEARCH]

Slide structure:
1. Title slide — Talk title, your name, conference name
2. Personal intro — Who you are and why you are qualified to speak on this
3. The Big Question — Pose the central question/challenge
4. Attention Hook — Surprising statistic, provocative statement, or story opening
5. Context / Why Now — Why this topic matters today
6-8. Argument 1 — Claim, evidence, example (3 slides)
9-11. Argument 2 — Claim, evidence, example (3 slides)
12-14. Argument 3 — Claim, evidence, example (3 slides)
15. The Story — Personal narrative that illustrates your thesis
16. Case Study — Real-world example with before/after
17. Counter-argument — Acknowledge the strongest objection and address it
18. Framework / Model — A visual framework the audience can take away
19. Practical Application #1 — What the audience can do tomorrow
20. Practical Application #2 — What the audience can do this month
21. Practical Application #3 — What the audience can do this quarter
22. Resources — Books, tools, links
23. The Big Takeaway — One sentence summary of the entire talk
24. Call to Action — What you want the audience to do
25. Thank You + Contact — Your contact info, social handles, QR code for slides

Design notes:
- Minimal text per slide (this is a talk, not a document)
- Heavy use of images, diagrams, and single statements
- Speaker notes should contain the full talking points
- Include "[PAUSE]" markers in speaker notes for dramatic effect
```

## Template 8: Investor Pitch Deck

```
Create a 12-slide investor pitch deck.

Company: [NAME]
Tagline: [ONE-SENTENCE DESCRIPTION]
Stage: [PRE-SEED / SEED / SERIES A / SERIES B+]
Industry: [SECTOR]
Founded: [YEAR]
Location: [CITY/COUNTRY]
Raising: [AMOUNT]
Pre-money valuation: [IF APPLICABLE]

Key metrics:
- Revenue: [MRR/ARR]
- Growth rate: [MONTH-OVER-MONTH OR YEAR-OVER-YEAR]
- Customers: [NUMBER]
- Team size: [NUMBER]
- Burn rate: [MONTHLY]
- Runway: [MONTHS]

Slide structure (Guy Kawasaki framework):
1. Title — Company name, tagline, contact info
2. Problem — The pain point you are solving (make it emotional and relatable)
3. Solution — Your product and how it solves the problem
4. Market Size — TAM, SAM, SOM with sources
5. Business Model — How you make money (pricing, unit economics)
6. Traction — Key metrics, growth chart, customer logos
7. Marketing and Sales — Go-to-market strategy, customer acquisition
8. Competition — Competitive landscape matrix (position yourself favorably)
9. Team — Founders and key hires with relevant experience
10. Financials — Revenue projections, key assumptions (3-year forecast)
11. The Ask — How much you are raising, use of funds breakdown
12. Vision — Where you are going (the big dream)

Design: Clean, minimal, data-rich. Every slide should have one key number.
Speaker notes should prepare for: "Why now?", "Why you?", "What if [competitor]?"
```

## Usage Tips

1. **Choose the template closest to your need** and customize the bracketed fields with your specific information.

2. **Combine templates** when needed. A project kickoff might borrow slides from the budget request template for the resource section.

3. **Always review AI output** against your actual knowledge. AI provides structure and draft content; your expertise provides accuracy and nuance.

4. **Iterate the output** by asking follow-up prompts: "Make slide 7 more data-heavy" or "Rewrite the speaker notes for a less formal tone."

5. **Save your best customized versions** as personal templates for future use.
