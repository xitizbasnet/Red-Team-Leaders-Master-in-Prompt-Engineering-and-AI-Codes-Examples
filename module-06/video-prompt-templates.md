# Video Generation Prompt Templates

## Module 06: Code and Examples

---

## How to Use These Templates

Each template provides a structured framework for generating AI video. Fill in the bracketed sections with your specific details. Templates are organized by platform and use case.

**Platform Key:**
- **S** = Optimized for Sora
- **R** = Optimized for Runway Gen-3
- **K** = Optimized for Kling
- **All** = Works well across platforms

---

## Template 1: Cinematic Establishing Shot [All]

### Structure
```
[TIME OF DAY] [WEATHER/ATMOSPHERE] over [LOCATION], [CAMERA MOVEMENT]
revealing [KEY SUBJECT/LANDMARK]. [ENVIRONMENTAL DETAILS]. [LIGHTING
DESCRIPTION]. Cinematic quality, [LENS/STYLE]. [PACING DESCRIPTION].
```

### Example A: City at Dawn
```
Golden dawn breaking over a foggy San Francisco skyline, slow drone
ascending from below the Golden Gate Bridge to reveal the entire bay.
Fog rolls between skyscraper tops like a white ocean. First rays of
sunlight catch the bridge cables creating golden lines. Cinematic
quality, shot on RED with anamorphic lens, gentle lens flare.
Majestic and slow-paced.
```

### Example B: Wilderness
```
Overcast twilight over an ancient Scottish highland landscape, slow
dolly forward through heather-covered hills. A lone stag stands on
a distant ridge, silhouetted against the gray sky. Mist drifts through
the valley. Moody, atmospheric lighting with deep shadows. Cinematic
quality, wide-angle lens. Contemplative and unhurried.
```

### Example C: Urban Night
```
Rain-soaked midnight in Tokyo's Shinjuku district, slow tracking shot
at eye level through a neon-lit alley. Reflections of red, blue, and
pink neon signs shimmer in puddles. Steam rises from street vents.
A few pedestrians with umbrellas pass by. Cyberpunk atmosphere,
anamorphic lens with natural flare. Moody, noir pacing.
```

## Template 2: Product Showcase [R, S]

### Structure
```
[CAMERA MOVEMENT] around [PRODUCT] on [SURFACE]. [PRODUCT DETAILS
VISIBLE]. [LIGHTING SETUP]. [ENVIRONMENTAL EFFECTS]. Premium
commercial quality. [STYLE/MOOD]. [DURATION/SPEED].
```

### Example A: Luxury Watch
```
Slow 180-degree orbit around a luxury chronograph watch on a dark
slate surface. The camera reveals the midnight blue dial, polished
steel bezel, and intricate crown detail. Dramatic studio lighting
with sharp reflections on polished metal. Water droplets slowly roll
down the crystal surface. Premium commercial quality. Sophisticated
and elegant mood. Slow, deliberate pace, 5 seconds.
```

### Example B: Sneaker
```
Dynamic low-angle tracking shot following a sneaker landing on wet
pavement. Slow motion water splash fans out from impact. Camera
continues to rise and orbit, revealing the shoe from multiple angles.
Colorful LED lights reflect off the wet surface. Urban commercial
quality with high-energy feel. Bold and dynamic. 4 seconds.
```

### Example C: Perfume
```
Macro close-up of a perfume bottle sprayer releasing a fine mist in
ultra slow motion. The mist catches warm backlight, creating golden
sparkle. Camera slowly pulls back to reveal the full bottle surrounded
by fresh flowers. Soft, diffused lighting. Premium beauty commercial
quality. Sensual and luxurious. 5 seconds.
```

## Template 3: Nature and Wildlife [S, K]

### Structure
```
[SHOT TYPE] of [ANIMAL/NATURAL PHENOMENON] in [HABITAT].
[SPECIFIC ACTION/BEHAVIOR]. [CAMERA BEHAVIOR]. [ENVIRONMENTAL
CONDITIONS]. [LIGHTING]. National Geographic quality. [EMOTIONAL TONE].
```

### Example A: Marine Life
```
Underwater medium shot of a sea turtle gracefully swimming through
a sunlit coral reef. Schools of tropical fish scatter and regroup
as the turtle passes. Camera follows alongside at a matching pace.
Crystal-clear tropical water with visible sun rays from above.
Natural underwater lighting with blue-green tones. National Geographic
quality. Peaceful and awe-inspiring.
```

### Example B: Birds in Flight
```
Slow-motion tracking shot of a bald eagle swooping down to catch a
fish from a river surface. Water explodes upward as talons strike.
Wings spread wide as the eagle rises with its catch. Camera follows
the arc of flight from slightly below. Early morning golden hour light.
National Geographic quality. Dramatic and powerful.
```

### Example C: Weather
```
Time-lapse of massive cumulonimbus storm clouds building and churning
over a wheat field. Lightning illuminates the cloud interiors. The
camera is low and static, looking up. Dramatic contrast between dark
storm and golden wheat. Storm chasing photography quality. Ominous
and awe-inspiring. 6 seconds covering 2 hours of real time.
```

## Template 8: Time-Lapse [S, K]

### Structure
```
[DURATION COMPRESSED] time-lapse of [SUBJECT/SCENE]. [WHAT CHANGES
OVER TIME]. [CAMERA POSITION]. [KEY MOMENTS TO CAPTURE]. [LIGHTING
PROGRESSION]. [STYLE]. [MOOD].
```

### Example A: Flower Blooming
```
24-hour time-lapse compressed into 5 seconds of a peony flower
blooming from tight bud to full open bloom. Petals slowly unfurl and
open. Camera holds steady in macro close-up. Morning dew evaporates
as light shifts from cool dawn to warm midday. Light changes naturally
throughout. Studio quality with natural lighting. Beautiful and
meditative.
```

### Example B: City Day-to-Night
```
12-hour time-lapse of a cityscape transitioning from midday to
midnight. Shadows sweep across buildings, the sky shifts from blue to
golden to purple to dark. City lights switch on one by one. Traffic
creates light trails on streets below. Camera is static on a high
rooftop. Golden hour is the visual climax. Hyperlapse quality. Dynamic
and mesmerizing.
```

### Example C: Construction
```
6-month time-lapse of a skyscraper being built from foundation to
completion. Steel beams rise, glass panels are installed, cranes
rotate. Workers appear and disappear like ants. Weather and seasons
change. Camera holds a static wide-angle view. From empty lot to
finished building. Documentary quality. Ambitious and satisfying.
```

## Platform-Specific Tips

### Sora Tips
```
- Describe the full narrative arc (what happens from start to finish)
- Include physical details and interactions
- Specify exact camera movements and transitions
- Mention lighting changes over the duration
- Works well with longer, more descriptive prompts
- Specify "photorealistic" or "cinematic" for best quality
- Can handle complex multi-element scenes
```

### Runway Gen-3 Tips
```
- Lead with camera movement description
- Keep prompts concise and focused (simpler than Sora)
- Use image-to-video for more control over the starting frame
- Leverage Motion Brush for precise regional motion control
- Specify camera movement type explicitly
- Works best with single, clear actions per clip
- Generate 5-second clips and edit together
```

### Kling Tips
```
- Good with human motion and expressions
- Describe the motion quality explicitly (smooth, dynamic, slow)
- Works well with image-to-video
- Can handle longer durations than some competitors
- Specify whether you want slow motion or real-time speed
- Include emotional descriptions for character-focused shots
- Generate multiple versions and select the best
```

## Combination Workflows

### Creating a 30-Second Commercial

```
Clip 1 (0-5s): Establishing shot
Template 1 → Generate with Sora for maximum quality

Clip 2 (5-10s): Product reveal
Template 2 → Generate with Runway for product control

Clip 3 (10-15s): Lifestyle use
Template 9 → Generate with Sora for natural human motion

Clip 4 (15-20s): Detail/feature shot
Template 2 variant → Generate with Runway (macro focus)

Clip 5 (20-25s): Emotional payoff
Template 4 → Generate with Sora for expression quality

Clip 6 (25-30s): Brand/logo
Template 10 → Generate with Runway for precise motion

Assembly:
- Edit together in Premiere Pro or DaVinci Resolve
- Apply consistent color grading across all clips
- Add music (Suno) and voiceover (ElevenLabs)
- Add text overlays and call to action
```
